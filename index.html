What is this about ?

Unlike regular hackathons, this is a 2-step challenge to see how far would you go to develop your idea in a totally new IoT environment. You will have the opportunity to use a new IoT platform (ACTIVE), developed as part of the EIT Digital High Impact Initiative.

More about ACTIVE

In the first part of the challenge that will span over a weekend, you along with your team, will learn about the platform and then come up with an idea you want to work on based on the themes of the challenge. At the end of the weekend you will present your idea / project proposal in terms of feasibility, practicality and viability and how it fits to the theme.

Themes

Themes

The focus for this event would be on Sustainable Cities.

Under this umbrella theme, we have identified the following sub-catagories :

Smart Energy

Smart Healthcare and Well-being

Smart Transportation and Infrastructure

Smart Consumer Electronics

Your idea should be based around these themes.

During the Stage 1 of the challenge you will have the opportunity to learn about the new ACTIVE IoT platform and you can shape your idea accordingly.

In addition to the platform, you can use any other open data/ open software as you require. If you are using proprietary software, make sure that you are legally allowed to use it for your project.

A jury decides the best ideas and then selected teams get a chance to proceed to the next stage where they work hands-on using the ACTIVE IoT Platform over a period of 1.5 months with guidance from experienced mentors, at the end of which the jury decides a winner.

Jury Criteria

Jury Criteria

The aim of this challenge is not just to build an application or a product but also for the teams to learn about the new ACTIVE IoT platform. Hence teams will be judged based on their understanding of the platform, originality of the idea, future applicability of their idea, how well and how extensively you use the platform, your feedback on the platform based on your usage, and of course, on your final presentations/demos.



Please note that if you are planning to enroll in the course II2300 Product Realisation Processes at KTH Kista by Prof. Mark Smith, you may also be able to get ECTS for the project you work on during the challenge, as part of the course. Contact us or Prof. Mark Smith for more details.

What's at stake here ?

The reward for stage 1 is obviously, the opportunity to take your idea forward to the next stage, where your team will be provided with mentorship and resources required to work on your idea.

The Grand prize for the final round :

1st prize - 7000 SEK

2nd prize - 4000 SEK

3rd prize - 2000 SEK

Also maybe the chance to continue working on your idea at EIT Digital - High Impact Initiative.

All participants get certificates for participation of course.
